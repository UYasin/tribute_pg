J.R.R Tolkien

The man who made Middle Earth

Here's a time line of Professor Tolkein's life:

  • 1892 - Born in Bloemfontein, South Africa.
  • 1908 - Aged 16, Tolkein met Edith Mary Bratt, his future wife. He was a student at the time and therefore he was not allowed to have contact with her for several years in order to focus on studies.
  • 1909 - Won an exhibition at Exeter College in Oxford where he studied English literature
  • 1913 - Married his childhood sweetheart Edith. His faithfulness and honesty to her prior to being wed would appear in his writing, most notably in the story of "Beren and Luthien"
  • 1916 - After completing his degree, Tolkien enlisted in the military and fought in the First World War.
  • 1917 - Begins work on "The Silmarillion". This epic lies at the heart of his most notable works. Rather than being plot driven this book explained The history of a universe. J.R.R. Tolkien would continuelay update this work until his death.
  • 1930 - Was inspired to write The Hobbit, his first published novel taking place in Middle-earth.
  • 1937 Published "The Hobbit" and recieved great commercial success.
  • 1945 - Was awarded the Merton Professorship and tasked with more teaching responsibilites, taking time away from writing.
  • 1954 - Published the first edition of "The Lord of the Rings" and surpassed the success of "The Hobbit"
  • 1965 - It wasn't until 11 years adfter the initial publication of "The Lord of the Rings" that the book would be published in America where it saw widespread commercial success making it an international bestseller.
  • 1968 - Moved from Oxford to Poole in order to have a little more privacy and to enjoy the simple things in life such as farmland, gardens, and trees.
  • 1971 - His wife of 55 years passes away. Tolkien Had the name Lúthien engraved on the tombstone.
  • 1972 - Was awarded the honorary degree of Doctor of Letters from Oxford University. An award bestowed on him not for his writing but rather for his researched on linguistic studies.
  • 1973 - Dies at the age of 81. Tolkien was buried next to his wife having the name Beren engraved into his tombstone to complement the one on Edith's tombstome.
